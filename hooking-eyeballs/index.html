Hooking Eyeballs on Social

10/13 by Bram Moreinis

I'm still trying to un-see a perfect image for this blog post if we were in a horror movie. But we're not, so instead I'll use a fishing lure photo to kick off pointers on writing posts that hook eyeballs (get the interest of readers) so they read the rest of it, and maybe even comment.

Blog History

Before Insta and Snap, before Facebook, were the blogs: "web logs" that served as open diaries or journals. Blogs got really big after WordPress let people create and share commentable blogs for free, which is why over 25% of all websites are made with Wordpress now.

But nothing is free. Time is money (if you weren't reading this blog you could be making money doing something else) and attention can be bought. Advertisers might, for example, put an ad for jighead lures here, because the title of the post made them think it would attract walleye fishermen.

Hooking Eyeballs

So bloggers learned that they had to sell their posts to readers. Here's what they found:

  1. First, grab eyeballs with click-bait:
    • An eye-catching image
    • A matching headline (title)
  2. Follow with a short lede:
    • Tie the image and title to a reader's interest.
    • Shift into what you want to write about.
  3. Don’t make them bounce!
    • Don’t bore the reader
    • Don’t be clickbaity (break a promise)
    • Don’t hit them with a wall of text

Sticky or Bouncy?

In web language, a page is "sticky" if it gets people to stay there and engage with it. If people see the page, scan briefly, and then leave before spending time there, they have "bounced". Which of these would you stick to, and which would you bounce from?

We'd have to rate three things, with 4 being super-sticky and 1 being super-bouncy:

  1. Sticky Featured Photo
  2. Sticky Headline
  3. Sticky Lede Paragraph
If your blog totals a 10 or more, it's a win!
